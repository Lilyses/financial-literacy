Lesson 1

Introduction to Money Management

What is Money Management?

Money management is the process of planning, controlling and managing the use of money wisely and carefully. It involves how a person acquires, spends, saves and also manages money to achieve financial goals.

It is especially important for students — pocket money needs to be used carefully so it doesn't run out before the end of the month.

Why many students struggle

  • Impulsive purchases without thinking
  • Spending too much on entertainment
  • Failing to save any amount monthly
  • No plan or budget to follow

Why it matters

  • Bad habits now = financial stress later
  • Good habits now = freedom later
  • Most students never learn this in school

Lesson 2

Needs vs Wants

✅ NEEDS — Must Have
  • 🍚 Food and groceries
  • 📚 School supplies
  • 🚌 Transportation to campus
  • 🏠 Accommodation
  • 📶 Internet access for learning
💛 WANTS — Nice to Have
  • 🎮 Unnecessary gadgets
  • 📺 Premium entertainment subscriptions
  • 👕 Trendy clothes or accessories
  • 🎫 Concert tickets
  • ☕ Fast food or coffee runs

"Can you live without it for ONE WEEK?"

✅ YES → It's a WANT
❌ NO → It's a NEED

Lesson 3

Common Money Management Mistakes

01

Not determining wants vs. needs

Most students overspend because they prioritize wants over needs, which leads to wasting money.

✔ Solution: Use the ONE WEEK test before buying.

02

Not having a plan for their money

No spending and savings plan leads to overspending and makes it harder to reach financial goals.

✔ Solution: Create a monthly budget (see next lesson).

03

Succumbing to peer pressure

Many students spend more than they can afford because they're influenced by friends.

✔ Solution: Suggest cheaper activities to friends.

04

Misuse of student loans

Students should only borrow for educational purposes to avoid a high debt burden later.

✔ Solution: Only borrow what is necessary for studies.

😟 Before — Poor habits

  • Money gone in 2 weeks
  • Stressed about finances
  • Borrow from friends

😊 After — Good habits

  • Money lasts all month
  • Peace of mind
  • Have savings, no debt

Lesson 4

Budgeting Basics

What is a budget?

A budget is a financial document used to project future income and expenses. It plans future saving and spending as well as planned income and expenses.

Why is it important?

Building a budget helps you control your spending habits. It helps you identify expenses that may be more than necessary so you can make smarter financial decisions.

📌 50/30/20 Rule

Monthly Allowance: RM800

NEEDS 50%
WANTS 30%
SAVE 20%
  • Needs (50%) = RM400 → Food, transport, rent
  • Wants (30%) = RM240 → Entertainment, shopping
  • Savings (20%) = RM160 → Emergency fund, future goals

🎬 Video Tutorial: 5 Steps to Create a Simple Budget Plan

▶

5 Steps to Create a Simple Budget

  1. Determine your income — calculate and total all sources of monthly income to know how much is available for spending and saving.
  2. Create a list of monthly expenses — list and categorize expenses like food and necessities so you know where the money goes.
  3. Set savings goals — decide how much to save each month, e.g. 10%–20% of your income.
  4. Differentiate needs and wants — prioritize essential expenses first and reduce spending on non-essentials.
  5. Track and adjust your budget — review regularly to keep spending under control and reach your goals.

Lesson 5

6 Smart Spending Tips for Students

TIP 1
🗂️Make a monthly spending plan

Divide money for needs, wants and savings separately.

TIP 2
🚫Avoid impulse buying

Think before you buy — don't be swayed by promotions.

TIP 3
📒Track every expense

Record daily spending to see where money really goes.

TIP 4
🌿Embrace minimalism

Buy only what you need — avoid excessive purchases.

TIP 5
🏷️Use student discounts wisely

Only buy out of necessity, not just because it's discounted.

TIP 6
⚖️Compare prices before purchasing

Check several stores or platforms before deciding.

📌 Bonus Tips

  • Cook at home instead of eating out (save RM10–15 per meal)
  • Buy used textbooks (save 50–70%)
  • Walk or bike to class when possible
  • Share subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify) with friends
  • Wait 24 hours before buying anything over RM50
  • Bring a water bottle (save RM3–5 daily)
  • Use public transport instead of driving
